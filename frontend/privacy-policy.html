FinSaathi Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Overview

FinSaathi is a financial literacy and safety application. It watches for risky money messages, payment prompts, and payment-app moments only after you give the required Android permissions. This policy explains what data we process, why we process it, and your choices.

FinSaathi gives safety warnings only. It does not give loans, sell investments, replace your bank, or ask for OTP, UPI PIN, Aadhaar, PAN, bank password, or card details. FinSaathi does not require or store an exact bank balance, and it does not fetch balances from your bank. If balance context is needed, FinSaathi uses a rough balance range only for safer guidance.

2. Data We Process

  • SMS data: incoming SMS may be locally scanned when SMS access is granted so FinSaathi can identify financial or risky messages. Relevant SMS sender IDs, parsed amounts, categories, risk flags, and link/domain signals may be processed.
  • Notification data: notification title/text from non-messaging apps may be locally inspected when notification access is granted so FinSaathi can identify payment, financial, or risky prompts before suppressing or classifying them.
  • Usage access data: foreground app events are used to detect supported UPI/payment apps and selected link-context apps such as WhatsApp when needed to time risk warnings.
  • Overlay alert data: alert title, message, risk level, and user action are processed when a safety warning is shown on screen, including high-risk, cashflow, setup, or fallback alerts.
  • Link and payment context: URL/domain signals, UPI deep-link signals, payee labels/handles, request type, and approximate amounts may be processed when available.
  • Profile and setup data: language, consent status, selected essential goals, optional rough money range/current-balance band, and permission state.
  • Feedback and diagnostics: usefulness feedback, app logs, event IDs, participant/device identifier, and delivery diagnostics.

3. Local-First Processing and Upload Boundaries

FinSaathi tries to classify messages and app events on the device first where possible. For normal cashflow alerts, the app sends parsed signals such as category, confidence, amount, timestamp, SMS sender ID, link/domain flags, and participant ID instead of sending the complete SMS text.

For payment-request, scam, account-access, or notification inspection, selected raw notification or payment-request text may be sent to the FinSaathi backend because the backend needs context to classify risk and generate a safer warning. Link context such as a raw URL, clicked URL, UPI deep link, or resolved domain can also be sent. If delivery fails, telemetry and feedback can be queued locally on the device and sent later when connectivity returns.

4. Why We Use This Data

  • To detect risky money messages, payment requests, suspicious links, and unsafe account-access prompts.
  • To show timely safety warnings before or during risky payment, cashflow, setup, or account-access moments.
  • To estimate safe spending thresholds and protect essential goals such as ration, rent, school, medicine, and transport.
  • To remember setup choices, consent status, language, and permission state.
  • To improve reliability, reduce false alerts, and measure whether warnings were useful.

5. Permissions

  • SMS: locally scan incoming SMS so FinSaathi can identify debit, UPI, scam, OTP, or unsafe access signals.
  • Notification Access: locally inspect non-messaging notification title/text to find payment, financial, or risky prompts.
  • Usage Access: detect when a UPI/payment app or selected link-context app is opened so warnings can appear at the right moment.
  • Overlay: show stop-and-verify safety warnings over the screen for important payment, cashflow, setup, fallback, or account-access moments.
  • Post Notifications: show safety alerts and fallback warnings.
  • Foreground Service and Boot: keep monitoring active after setup and after device restart when the user has enabled monitoring.

6. What We Do Not Collect or Ask For

  • We do not ask for OTP, UPI PIN, Aadhaar, PAN, bank password, or card details.
  • We do not fetch bank balances directly from your bank. Exact bank balance is not required or stored; only rough balance ranges are used.
  • We do not sell loans, investments, insurance, or financial products.
  • We do not sell personal data.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data. Data may be processed by infrastructure providers required to run the service, such as hosting, logging, analytics, or database providers. These providers are used only to operate FinSaathi and improve safety, reliability, and support.

8. Retention and Deletion

App data is retained only as needed for safety guidance, diagnostics, research validation, support, and system reliability. You can request deletion by contacting support. You can also turn off SMS, notification, usage, overlay, or notification permissions from Android settings at any time.

9. Security

We use HTTPS for data sent between the app and backend, restrict release builds to secure transport, and use operational controls to protect data during processing. No app can remove every risk, so FinSaathi is designed to warn and slow down risky actions rather than guarantee fraud prevention.
